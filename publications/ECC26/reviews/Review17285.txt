Reviewer 10 of ECC 2026 submission 701

Comments to the author
======================

The paper presents a well-developed and practically
relevant framework for change-point detection that is based
on truncated online DMD with control. The combination of
online DMDc updates, rank-truncated online SVD, and a
reconstruction-error ratio statistic is technically very
appropriate and very well motivated. The industrial case
studies (nonlinear two-tank system and BESS data) greatly
strengthen the applied contribution of the paper and prove
that the method can be instantly used in control-oriented
environments. 
Several aspects of the paper are particularly strong: the
attention to numerical stability, the explicit discussion
of computational complexity, and the interpretability of
the detection metric. These features will be valuable for
online monitoring, system identification, and industrial
fault detection.
However, some parts of the exposition are quite verbose and
could really do with "tight" treatment. The theoretical
sections are right but dense, and some transitions between
derivations (particularly the basis-alignment steps and
online SVD integration) assume substantial prior knowledge,
which may limit readability. Similarly, the experimental
section is well constructed but could be more concise
without losing impact.
I also suggest shortening the paper down to the suggested
6-page length of ECC site.
