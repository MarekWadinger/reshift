\section{Biography}
\begin{IEEEbiography}
    [{\includegraphics[width=1in,height=1.25in,clip,keepaspectratio]{biography/wadinger.jpg}}]{Marek Wadinger} conducted the presented research during his research stay at Osaka University. He is currently pursuing a Ph.D. in process control at STUBA\@. His research interests include anomaly detection, reliability engineering, and streaming data analysis. He has co-authored several publications, such as ``Real-Time Outlier Detection with Dynamic Process Limits'' and ``Adaptable and Interpretable Framework for Anomaly Detection in SCADA-based Industrial Systems.'' He was also affiliated with the TESLA Group, a.s., as an AI specialist.
\end{IEEEbiography}
\begin{IEEEbiography}
    [{\includegraphics[width=1in,height=1.25in,clip,keepaspectratio]{biography/kvasnica.png}}]{Prof. Michal Kvasnica}
    received his Ph.D. degree at ETH Zurich, Switzerland, in 2008. Since 2012, he has been a tenured associate professor at STUBA's Department of Information Engineering and Process Control and promoted to full professor in 2022. He served as the Head of the Department of Information Engineering and Process Control from 2019 to 2023. His research focuses on model predictive control, hybrid systems, and microgrid control. Prof. Kvasnica has authored prestigious publications and is an active contributor to the academic community.
\end{IEEEbiography}
\begin{IEEEbiography}
    [{\includegraphics[width=1in,height=1.25in,clip,keepaspectratio]{biography/kawahara.jpg}}]{Prof. Yoshinobu Kawahara}
    received his Ph.D. degree from The University of Tokyo in 2008. After completing his doctoral studies, he served as an Associate Professor at the Osaka University, from 2013. Since 2019, he served as full Professor at the Kyushu University and since 2022 at the Osaka University. Prof. Kawahara is also the Team Director of the Structured Learning Team at the RIKEN Center for Advanced Intelligence Project. His research interests encompass machine learning, dynamical systems, and nonlinear systems.
\end{IEEEbiography}
